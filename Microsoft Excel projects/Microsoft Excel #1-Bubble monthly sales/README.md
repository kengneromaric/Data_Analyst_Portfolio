# Microsoft Excel #1-Bubble monthly sales

Bubble had a hard time determining the optimal inventory and staffing levels.

Since their sales rely on tourists, their demand varies based on tourism seasons. They were struggling to anticipate how much inventory to order and how many staff to have available to manage the shop.

They typically experienced periods of overstaffing and others of understaffing, as well as moments where there was excess stock, and others with exhausted stock.

They couldn’t keep up with this uncertainty, and it was crippling their business. Therefore, the manager reached out to me to analyze their sales data and help them understand how to manage the fluctuations over the year.

Once I got hold of their past 3 years’ sales data, I organized it by month and analyzed it.

I created a visualization to identify trends over the years as well as the fluctuations in sales over the seasons.

I shared my findings with the manager and my suggestions on how they should prepare themselves with respect to the period of the year to stay efficient in their staffing and inventory to cut losses.

Now, they are more prepared for the upcoming year and are able to forecast periods of high and low and adjust accordingly.

They can also offer a better customer experience since they are more prepared.
